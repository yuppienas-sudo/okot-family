Our Family History

Journey Through Generations of the Okot Legacy

History Timeline

Take a clear, chronological tour through the Okot family’s major milestones from its origins to the present day.

Early 1800s

The Origins

The Okot family traces its roots to the Acholi region of Northern Uganda. Our ancestors were respected community leaders, known for their wisdom and dedication to their people.

1880s

The Founding Father

Chief Okot Oloo established the family settlement in what is now known as Okot Village, Gulu District. He was known for his fair leadership and commitment to family unity.

1920-1960

Growth & Expansion

The family grew significantly during this period, with members spreading across Uganda while maintaining strong ties to their ancestral home. Education became a priority.

1970-1990

Challenges & Resilience

Despite the challenges of political instability, the Okot family remained united. Many family members contributed to community rebuilding and peace efforts.

1990-Present

Global Family

Today, Okot family members can be found across Uganda, Kenya, the UK, USA, and Canada. We continue to celebrate our heritage while embracing our global presence.

Cultural Heritage & Traditions

Language

We preserve the Acholi language, passing down traditional proverbs and stories to younger generations.

Music, Dance & Drama

Traditional Acholi music, dance, and theatrical storytelling remain an important part of our family celebrations and gatherings.

Cuisine

Family recipes for traditional dishes like malakwang, boo, and groundnut sauce are cherished and shared.

Respect for Elders

Elders are honored as keepers of wisdom and history, playing a central role in family decisions.
